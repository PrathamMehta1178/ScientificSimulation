Pendulum

Pendulums showcase key principles of physics:

  • Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM): Pendulums oscillate back and forth in a repeating pattern, following SHM.
  • Energy Conservation: They convert between kinetic and potential energy as they swing, demonstrating energy conservation.
  • Gravity Influence: Gravity acts as a restoring force, pulling the pendulum back towards its equilibrium position.
  • Period Length Dependency: The period of a pendulum depends on its length but is relatively independent of its mass.
  • and Friction: Real pendulums experience damping due to air resistance and friction, causing their motion to slow over time.
Overall, pendulums provide tangible examples of fundamental physics concepts like motion, energy, and gravity.
