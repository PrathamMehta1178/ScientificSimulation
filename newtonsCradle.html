Newton's Cradle demonstrates key physics principles:

  • Conservation of Momentum: Momentum is transferred through the balls, keeping the total momentum constant.
  • Conservation of Energy: Energy is conserved as it transfers from potential to kinetic energy and back.
  • Elastic Collisions: The balls undergo nearly elastic collisions, conserving kinetic energy and momentum.
  • Transfer of Forces: Forces are transferred through the balls, showing Newton's third law of motion.
Overall, it illustrates how momentum and energy are conserved and transferred in a system.
