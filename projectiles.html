Projectiles

Projectiles showcase key principles of physics:

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: Projectiles follow Newton's laws, including continuous motion unless acted on by gravity.
  • Parabolic Trajectory: Projectiles follow a curved, parabolic path due to gravity.
  • Independence of Motion: Horizontal and vertical motions are independent; horizontal motion is constant, while vertical motion is uniformly accelerated by gravity.
  • Equations of Motion: Projectile motion is described by kinematic equations for horizontal and vertical distances.
  • Energy Conservation: Total mechanical energy (kinetic and potential) is conserved in the absence of air resistance.
These principles illustrate motion, forces, trajectories, and energy conservation.
