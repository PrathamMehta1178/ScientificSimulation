Collision Simulation

Collision demonstrates key physics principles:

  • Conservation of Momentum: Total momentum is conserved in all collisions.
  • Types of collisions
    • Elastic Collisions: Both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved.
    • Inelastic Collisions: Momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not.
  • Impulse and Force: Impulse (force over time) changes an object's momentum.
  • Newton's Third Law: Forces during collisions are equal and opposite.
  • Energy Transformation: Kinetic energy can convert to heat, sound, or deformation.
  • Center of Mass Motion: The center of mass's motion stays constant if no external forces act.
These principles illustrate momentum and energy conservation, force interactions, and Newtonian mechanics.
